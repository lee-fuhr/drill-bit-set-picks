A good drill bit set is the backbone of any toolbox. Whether you are hanging shelves, building furniture, or doing emergency repairs, having sharp, properly sized bits saves time and frustration. The trick is finding a kit that balances variety with quality: too few bits means frequent store trips, while cheap bits dull fast or snap under pressure. When shopping, look for sets with at least 29 pieces covering 1/16-inch to 1/2-inch, ideally with a 135-degree split point for faster starts and reduced walking. Titanium nitride or black oxide coatings help bits last longer, and a hard carrying case keeps everything organized.
